Week 1: Hands-on Exercise

Overview

Task: Complete the exercises, take screenshots of your work, and submit by due date.

Purpose: Demonstrate your ability to identify resources and utility on your native operating system.

Instructions

Part 1: Open the Task Manager on your computer

  • Open the task manager (Windows/PC), activity monitor (Mac/Apple), or whichever similar interface the operating system on your computer uses to display current processes and resource utilization.

    This may require some individual searching and research if you do not know how to do this on your current computer and operating system.

Part 2: Identify the programs and processes currently running

  • Within the Task Manager/Activity Monitor, find a list of current processes running for whichever OS you are running natively.
    • Take a screenshot of the currently running processes.

Part 3: Identify the amount of RAM currently being used and capacity

  • Also within the Task Manager/Activity Monitor, identify how much RAM is currently being used and the capacity on your computer.
    • Take a screenshot of your current RAM utilization and capacity.

Part 4: Identify total and available hard drive space

  • Find and determine the current free space and the total capacity of your main hard drive.
    • Take a screenshot of your hard drive capacity and free space.

Part 5: Submission

Submit your three screenshots in one document. Include your name and the date.

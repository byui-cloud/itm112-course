Week 13: Final Team Presentation

Overview

Prerequisite: Join a team.

Task: Create a Linux solution and create a presentation for a business executive team that might give approval and budget to solve a problem using Linux.

Purpose: Demonstrate your communication skills and ability to create a Linux solution.

Instructions

Overview

One part of the final for this course will be an in-person team presentation (only online courses: online video recording presentation as a team) of a Linux topic as a team. You will join a team of 3-6 and the team will select a Linux topic to present on.

Part 1: Linux Solution

You will select any of the Linux technologies (ie. web server, file server, remote desktop, etc.) learned this semester in class. Then create a fictitious company with a problem that can be solved by using this technology. You will present a business case for the problem being solved and then present why this Linux technology is an optimal solution for the company's problem.

Part 2: Presentation Details

Your team will create a business use case that will need to be solved and provide a solution using Linux services. The presentation audience will be a business executive team that is giving approval and budget to solve the problem. Your team is making the case for justification of the Linux solution as the best fit and best of class to solve the problem.

Present a cost analysis breakdown, evaluation of reliability and availability, and competitive matrix of other non-Linux solutions that compete with the technology you selected.

  • The presentation should be between 4-6 minutes long.
  • 6 minutes is the max and points will be deducted for going over or not covering the minimum 4 minutes.
  • Make sure to cover each of these areas:
    • Cost Analysis
    • Reliability and Availability
    • Competitive Matrix (including at least one comparison to non-Linux alternative)
  • Business attire clothing worn by all team members. The dress standard will be business class like you would wear appropriate to an actual business presentation to executives.
  • Professional presentation slides.
  • Each team member presents a portion of the presentation.

Part 3: Submission

Each person needs to provide one document that includes:

  • Your name and team number/name.
  • I participated in preparing and presenting my team's presentation: [TRUE OR FALSE]
  • Any research references used for your presentation.
  • Team Member Evaluation:
    1. Each team member's name with a comment about their contributions and participation with the presentation

Additionally, each person will submit:

  • Your presentation slides (PowerPoint, Google Slides, PDF, etc.).
  • Your presentation video recording (only online courses).
